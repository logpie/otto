When TimestampSigner.unsign() encounters a token with an extremely large or corrupted timestamp, it crashes with an unhandled ValueError (year is out of range) instead of raising BadTimeSignature. This happens because timestamp_to_datetime() calls datetime.fromtimestamp() which raises ValueError for timestamps that would result in dates beyond Python's datetime range. Fix the unsign() method in src/itsdangerous/timed.py to catch ValueError from timestamp_to_datetime() and convert it to a BadTimeSignature exception with a "Malformed timestamp" message.
